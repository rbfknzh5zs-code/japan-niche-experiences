Overview

Deep in the mountains of Nagano Prefecture lies a cedar forest that has stood for over 500 years. No roads lead here. No cell towers reach this valley. This is where you'll spend three days in complete silence ‚Äî with no phone, no Wi-Fi, and no distractions. Just the sound of wind through ancient trees, birdsong at dawn, and your own breathing during morning meditation.

This retreat combines the Japanese practice of shinrin-yoku (forest bathing) with zen meditation, campfire tea ceremony, and soaking in a hidden mountain onsen known only to the village elders. Your guide is a former monk who spent 10 years in a Kyoto zen temple before returning to the mountains to share this practice.
